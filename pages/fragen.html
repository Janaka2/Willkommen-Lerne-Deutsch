❓ Alle W-Fragen — mit System

Question words feel confusing because a few German W-words look like the wrong English word. Once you see the pattern and drill the traps, they stick for good. Read top to bottom, then practise at the end.

💡 Der eine Trick: WH → W

English question words start with Wh-. German ones start with W-. Same job — just drop the "h". They line up almost perfectly:

Who→Wer What→Was Where→Wo When→Wann Why→Warum Which→Welcher How→Wie
Even "how" fits
English "how" is the odd one out — but in German it's still a W-word: Wie. So the rule has no exceptions: every German question word starts with W.

⚠️ Die 4 Fallen (the false friends)

This is the whole problem in one box. These four look like an English word they do not mean. Learn these and the confusion is over:

Wer = WHO ✗ not "where"
Wo = WHERE ✗ not "who"
Wie = HOW ✗ not "why"
Wann = WHEN ✗ not "want"
🧠 Memory hooks
  • WeR = who → has an R for a peRson.
  • WO = where → the O is a little spot on the map = lOcation.
  • WIE = how → think "Wie geht's?" = How are you? (your #1 daily question).
  • WaRUM = why → answer needs a reason: "because of the RUM!" 🥃

📍 Die Wo-Familie: Ort & Richtung

German splits "where" into three by direction. Build them from wo: add -hin (going away →) or -her (coming toward ←).

📍
Wo?
Where? (no movement)
➡️
Wohin?
Where to? (wo + hin)
⬅️
Woher?
Where from? (wo + her)
Wo wohnst du? — Where do you live?
Wohin gehst du? — Where are you going (to)?
Woher kommst du? — Where do you come from?

🧠 Die Lern-Strategie (so bleibt es im Kopf)

  1. Lerne den Satz, nicht das Wort. Don't memorise "Wo = where". Memorise the whole question "Wo wohnst du?". The word comes back glued to a real sentence.
  2. Drill only the 4 traps (Wer/Wo/Wie/Wann) until they're automatic — that's 80% of the confusion.
  3. Say it loud, with the answer. Ask and answer: "Woher kommst du? — Ich komme aus Sri Lanka."
  4. Practise daily, 2 minutes. Use the Trap-Trainer below, then the Lernkarten for spaced repetition.

B1 Indirekte Fragen — höflich & weich

At B1 you wrap a question inside a polite frame. The little catch: the verb jumps to the end, just like in a Nebensatz.

Wo ist der Bahnhof?
direct: Where is the station?
Können Sie mir sagen, wo der Bahnhof ist?
indirect: Could you tell me where the station is?
Wissen Sie, wann der Zug abfährt?
Do you know when the train leaves?
Ja/Nein-Fragen → ob
A yes/no question has no question word, so use ob (whether):
Ich möchte wissen, ob der Laden noch offen ist. — I'd like to know whether the shop is still open.

Useful frames: Können Sie mir sagen, …? · Wissen Sie, …? · Ich möchte wissen, …? · Darf ich fragen, …?
