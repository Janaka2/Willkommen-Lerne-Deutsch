Nominativ · Akkusativ · Dativ · Genitiv

🧩 Die 4 Fälle (the 4 cases)

Cases tell you the role of a noun in the sentence. The noun barely changes — it's mostly the article in front that changes. Learn these tables and you've cracked the heart of German.

Die vier Fragen (the four questions)

1. Nominativ — Wer? Was?
The subject (who does it).
Der Mann schläft. — The man sleeps.
2. Akkusativ — Wen? Was?
The direct object.
Ich sehe den Mann. — I see the man.
3. Dativ — Wem?
The indirect object (receiver).
Ich helfe dem Mann. — I help the man.
4. Genitiv — Wessen?
Possession (of the…).
Das Auto des Mannes. — The man's car.

Ein Satz mit allen Fällen

Der Mann gibt der Frau das Buch seines Sohnes.

Der Mann = Nominativ (Wer?)
The man (subject)
der Frau = Dativ (Wem?)
to the woman (receiver)
das Buch = Akkusativ (Was?)
the book (direct object)
seines Sohnes = Genitiv (Wessen?)
of his son (possession)

→ "The man gives the woman his son's book."

B1 Die Fälle auf B1-Niveau

At B1 the same four cases drive two new structures. The case still comes from the word's job in the sentence — only the forms get richer.

Relativpronomen nach Fall

A relative pronoun takes the case of its job inside the relative clause.
